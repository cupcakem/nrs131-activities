Feeding Cues & Normal Patterns

Recognizing hunger, fullness, and adequate intake

⏱ Normal Feeding Frequency
8–12
feedings per 24 hrs
  • Feed on demand — not on a schedule
  • Avoid watching the clock
  • Cluster feeding is normal, especially evenings
  • Newborns have tiny stomachs — frequent feeds are physiologic
👁 WATCH THE BABY — not the clock
🍼 Hunger Cues — Early to Late
Key teaching point: Crying is a late feeding cue. By the time the baby is crying, they are already stressed and upset — comfort the baby first, then attempt to latch.
👅

Licking Lips

Early cue — baby is beginning to feel hunger

↔️

Rooting

Turning head side to side, opening mouth, searching for the nipple

✊

Hand to Mouth

Bringing fist or fingers to mouth — a clear mid-stage cue

😭

Crying — LATE CUE

Comfort the baby first. Attempting to latch a crying baby is very difficult.

😌 Fullness Cues vs. Hunger Cues

🍽 Hunger Cues

  • Rooting / turning head
  • Licking lips / smacking
  • Hand to mouth
  • Increased alertness
  • Fussiness (escalating)
  • Crying (late)

✅ Fullness / Satiety Cues

  • Releases breast / turns away
  • Relaxed hands and arms
  • Decreased sucking
  • Falls asleep at breast
  • Appears content, relaxed
  • No longer rooting
💩 Stool Progression — Signs of Adequate Intake

Meconium

Days 1–2
Black and sticky
Sterile; first bowel output

Transitional

Days 3–4
Brown to greenish-brown
Milk is transitioning

Mature

Day 5+
Mustard yellow, "seedy"
Sign milk is in!

📊 Assessing for Adequate Intake

👶 Weight

Up to 7–10% weight loss in first few days is expected. Should return to birth weight by 10–14 days.

💧 Wet Diapers

Expect 6+ wet diapers/day by day 4–5. Pale yellow urine is a good sign.

💩 Stool Output

Transition from meconium to yellow seedy stool by day 4–5 confirms milk transfer.

😊 Baby's Behavior

Waking for feeds, appearing satisfied after feeding, good skin color and tone.

🔊 Swallowing

Audible swallowing during feeding is a reliable indicator of milk transfer.

✔ Knowledge Check
