POST-BIRTH Warning Signs

AWHONN Evidence-Based Discharge Education · Know When to Call 911

⚠️

Most women recover without complications — but any woman can have a life-threatening postpartum complication. The leading causes of preventable maternal death include hemorrhage, VTE/PE, preeclampsia, cardiac disease, and infection. Teaching patients to recognize warning signs and act immediately saves lives.

P

Pain in the Chest

→ Cardiac disease · Pulmonary embolism

Chest pain or discomfort is never normal postpartum. May indicate PE (clot in lung) or peripartum cardiomyopathy. Cardiac disease accounts for 33% of all pregnancy-related deaths.

Associated symptoms: heart palpitations, feeling faint, discomfort that spreads to arm or jaw.

🚨 Call 911 immediately

Patient teaching: "It is not normal to have chest pain or pressure after having a baby. Do not wait — call 911."

O

Obstructed Breathing

→ Pulmonary embolism · Cardiac disease

Shortness of breath, shallow rapid respirations, or chest pain that worsens with coughing are not normal postpartum. PE is a leading cause of preventable maternal death — in most cases, SOB was not evaluated appropriately.

30% of PE patients have no prior evidence of DVT.

🚨 Call 911 immediately

Patient teaching: "If you feel like you can't catch your breath or it hurts to breathe — call 911, do not drive yourself."

S

Seizures

→ Eclampsia · Postpartum preeclampsia

Eclamptic seizures can occur up to 6 weeks postpartum. 26% occur beyond 48 hours post-delivery. 78% of postpartum eclampsia patients had no prior diagnosis of pregnancy-related hypertension.

Seizure is always an emergency regardless of known hypertension history.

🚨 Call 911 immediately

Patient teaching: "A seizure after having a baby is an emergency. Call 911 — do not try to drive to the hospital."

T

Thoughts of Harming Self or Baby

→ Postpartum depression · Postpartum psychosis

Postpartum depression affects 1 in 7 women. Postpartum psychosis, while rare, is a psychiatric emergency. Any thoughts of harming self or baby require immediate evaluation.

PPD can onset as early as 1 week and up to 1 year postpartum. Use Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale — score ≥12 indicates high risk.

🚨 Seek immediate help

Patient teaching: "These feelings are not your fault and you are not a bad mother. Please tell someone — your provider, a family member, or call 988."

B

Bleeding

→ Postpartum hemorrhage · Uterine atony

PPH accounts for 12–13% of all maternal deaths. Excess bleeding = any of the following:

▸ Soaks through more than 1 pad per hour
▸ Clots the size of an egg or larger
▸ Bright red bleeding that does not slow

Normal lochia: rubra (days 1–3) → serosa (days 4–10) → alba (days 10+). Foul-smelling lochia suggests infection.

📞 Call provider / 911 if no response

Patient teaching: "Some bleeding is normal — but soaking a pad every hour or passing large clots is not. Call immediately."

I

Incision Not Healing

→ Wound infection · Dehiscence

Applies to C-section incision or episiotomy/perineal repair. Warning signs include:

▸ Redness, warmth, swelling, or drainage
▸ Incision opening or pulling apart
▸ Odor from the wound
▸ Increasing pain at the site

Some serous drainage is expected; purulent, foul-smelling drainage is not.

📞 Call provider

Patient teaching: "Your incision should look better each day — not worse. Increasing redness or any drainage with odor means call your provider."

R

Red, Swollen, Painful Leg

→ Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)

VTE accounts for 9% of maternal deaths; at least half of VTE deaths had a strong chance of being prevented. Warning signs of DVT:

▸ Calf pain, redness, warmth, or swelling
▸ More swelling in one leg than the other
▸ Pain that is not improving

Leg pain is NOT a normal side effect of childbirth — even with an epidural.

📞 Call provider immediately

Patient teaching: "If one leg is more swollen, red, or painful than the other — do not massage it. Call your provider or go to the ED."

T

Temperature ≥ 100.4°F (38°C)

→ Infection · Endometritis · Mastitis

Fever postpartum indicates infection until proven otherwise. Sources include endometritis, wound infection, UTI, mastitis, or sepsis.

Associated symptoms: chills, body aches, flu-like symptoms, foul-smelling vaginal discharge, increased wound redness or drainage.

📞 Call provider

Patient teaching: "A temperature of 100.4°F or higher after leaving the hospital is not normal. Take your temperature if you feel chilled or have body aches — and call your provider."

H

Headache Unrelieved by Medication

→ Postpartum preeclampsia · Stroke

A severe headache that doesn't improve with medication, or any headache with vision changes, is a red flag for postpartum preeclampsia. Systolic BP ≥160 is linked to cerebral hemorrhage.

Additional warning signs: seeing spots or flashing lights, swelling of hands or face, RUQ pain, confusion.

🚨 Call provider / 911 if no response

Patient teaching: "A bad headache that Tylenol doesn't touch — especially with blurry vision — is a medical emergency. Call your provider now."

📋 What to Tell Every Patient at Discharge

1

Call your provider first

For most warning signs — call your OB or midwife. If no answer within a few minutes, go to the ED or call 911.

2

Call 911 immediately for

Chest pain, difficulty breathing, seizure, or thoughts of harming self or baby. Do not drive yourself.

3

Tell them you just had a baby

Always disclose your delivery date when seeking any medical care within one year of delivery — including the ED.

4

Post the handout somewhere visible

AWHONN recommends placing the POST-BIRTH "Save Your Life" handout on the refrigerator or somewhere easily accessible.
