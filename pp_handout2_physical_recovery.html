✅ Normal

  • Firm, round, midline
  • Descends ~1 cm/day
  • Non-palpable by ~day 10
  • Afterpains — especially with breastfeeding

⚠️ Abnormal — Report

  • Boggy (soft, not firm)
  • Deviated to one side (bladder distension)
  • Not descending as expected
  • Fundus rising (subinvolution)
⚠️ Avoid knee-chest position until at least 3rd postpartum week — the still-open uterus can allow air entry and risk air embolism.
🩸 Lochia

Lochia Rubra

Days 1–3
Dark red, fleshy odor. Bloody with decidua, mucus, fetal lanugo.

Lochia Serosa

Days 4–10
Pink to brownish. Thinner. Serosanguineous with leukocytes.

Lochia Alba

Day 10 – Week 6
Yellowish-white. Mostly leukocytes and mucus. Decreasing.

✅ Normal Lochia

  • Progresses through the three stages
  • Fleshy (not foul) odor
  • Amount decreases over time
  • May increase briefly with activity

⚠️ Abnormal — Report

  • Soaks >1 pad/hour
  • Clots ≥ egg-sized
  • Foul or offensive odor
  • Returns to rubra after serosa/alba
  • Absent lochia (may indicate retained products)
🔪 C-Section Incision Care

Patient Teaching Points

✅ Do

  • Shower as normal — pat dry
  • Let Steri-strips fall off naturally
  • Monitor daily for changes
  • Lift nothing heavier than the baby
  • Use pillow to splint when coughing/laughing

🚫 Don't

  • Apply creams or powders to incision
  • Remove Steri-strips manually
  • Lift the baby in the car seat
  • Submerge in bath or pool until healed
  • Ignore increasing pain at site
⚠️ "Not the baby in the car seat" — a common oversight. The car seat + baby together is far heavier than the baby alone and can stress the incision and abdominal muscles significantly.
🚿 Elimination

Urination

Before Discharge

  • Patient must void without difficulty
  • Assess for urinary retention
  • First void may be uncomfortable
  • Peribottle for comfort

Reducing UTI Risk

  • 8 × 8 oz glasses of water daily
  • Empty bladder frequently
  • Wipe front to back
  • Wash hands frequently
Teach S/S of UTI: burning, frequency, urgency, cloudy or foul-smelling urine, pelvic pain. Postpartum women are at increased risk due to perineal trauma, urinary catheterization, and decreased sensation.

Bowel Function

Expected

  • First BM by day 2–3
  • Hemorrhoids common
  • Stool softeners routinely offered
  • Fear of BM with perineal repair is normal

Interventions

  • Adequate hydration
  • High fiber diet
  • Early ambulation
  • Witch hazel pads for hemorrhoids
  • Sitz baths for comfort
⚖️ Weight, Fatigue & Activity

Expected Weight Loss & Recovery Timeline

Weight Loss Pattern

  • ~10–12 lbs immediately after birth
  • ~5 more lbs in first week (fluid)
  • Back to pre-pregnancy weight by 6–8 weeks (varies)
  • Target: no more than 1–2 lbs/week loss

Fatigue Management

  • 6 weeks for recovery — protect that time
  • Sleep when the baby sleeps
  • Limit visitors or put them to work
  • Accept help with meals and housework
  • Energy depletion from delivery + sleep deprivation is cumulative

✔ Knowledge Check


Yes — expected. The fundus descends approximately 1 cm per day. By day 3, a fundal height of 3 cm below the umbilicus is within normal limits. Firm and midline are reassuring findings.

Return to rubra after serosa or alba is abnormal. It may indicate subinvolution, retained placental fragments, or excessive activity. Advise her to rest and call the provider immediately for evaluation. If she is soaking a pad per hour or has clots, go to the ED.
