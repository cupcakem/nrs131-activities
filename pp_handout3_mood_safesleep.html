💜 Postpartum Mood — Blues vs. Depression

Postpartum Blues

Normal · Self-limiting
  • Peaks days 3–5 postpartum
  • Resolves within 2 weeks
  • Mood swings, tearfulness
  • Crying over commercials
  • Feeling overwhelmed but functional
  • Related to hormonal shifts

Reassurance and support are appropriate. No treatment required if resolving within 2 weeks.

Postpartum Depression

Affects 1 in 7 women · Requires treatment
  • Can onset 1 week to 1 year postpartum
  • Does not resolve on its own
  • Persistent, pervasive sadness
  • Impairs ability to care for self or baby
  • Requires medical evaluation and treatment
  • Multiple risk factors increase severity

PPD is not a character flaw or weakness — it is a medical condition requiring treatment.

⚠️ Warning Signs — When Blues Become Something More

Crying excessively, most of the day
Significant change in appetite
Feeling helpless or hopeless
Extreme worry or anxiety
Unable to sleep or sleeping all the time
Trouble bonding with baby
Unable to care for self or baby
Panic attacks
Fear of harming self or baby
Feeling loss of control

Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS)

The EPDS is a validated 10-item self-report tool used to screen for PPD. Score ≥12 indicates high risk and warrants referral for mental health evaluation and counseling. All postpartum patients should be screened — PPD can develop up to one year after delivery. Breastfeeding has been shown to help reduce postpartum depression risk.

😴 Safe Sleep for Newborns

✅ DO — Safe Sleep Practices

  • Place baby on their back for every sleep
  • Use a flat, firm sleep surface (crib, bassinet)
  • Fitted sheet only — nothing else in crib
  • Room-share (same room, different sleep surface) for first 6–12 months (AAP)
  • Keep room at a comfortable temperature
  • Use a pacifier at sleep time (after BF established)

🚫 DON'T — Unsafe Sleep

  • Never bed-share — present in 3 out of 5 SUID deaths; AAP states bed-sharing nearly triples the risk of sudden unexpected infant death (AAP 2022)
  • No sleep positioners, wedges, or anti-roll pillows (FDA warning)
  • No loose bedding, bumpers, stuffed animals, pillows
  • Don't allow baby to sleep in car seat, swing, or carrier
  • No drop-side cribs or cribs with missing parts
  • No soft surfaces (sofa, waterbed, cushion)
😢 Period of PURPLE Crying

What is the Period of PURPLE Crying?

Peak of crying
Unexpected
Resists soothing
Pain-like face
Long lasting
Evening clustering

This is a normal developmental phase beginning around 2 weeks of age, peaking around 2 months, and resolving by 3–5 months. The baby is not sick or in danger. Educating parents about this phase is a primary prevention strategy for Shaken Baby Syndrome — the #1 trigger is inconsolable crying.

🚨 Shaken Baby Syndrome — Never Shake a Baby

Shaking causes severe and permanent brain damage or death. There is no "safe" amount of shaking. If a caregiver feels overwhelmed: put the baby down in a safe place, walk away, and take a break. Call a trusted person for help. It is always better to let a baby cry safely than to cause permanent harm.

📞 Sex, Contraception & Follow-Up
  • Nothing in the vagina for 6 weeks — intercourse, tampons, douching
  • Ovulation can resume as early as 2 weeks postpartum
  • Pregnancy can occur before the first postpartum period — have a contraception plan before discharge
  • If breastfeeding: confirm contraceptive method is compatible (avoid combined estrogen-progestin methods early)
  • Postpartum follow-up visit: within 6 weeks — schedule before discharge
  • Remind patients to disclose recent delivery to any provider they see within one year
